## Overview

Lichess Puzzles is a comprehensive tactical training system featuring millions of chess puzzles generated from real games. Each puzzle represents a critical moment where one side can achieve a significant advantage or deliver checkmate.

<Note>
  All puzzles come from actual Lichess games, ensuring realistic tactical scenarios you'll encounter in your own play.
</Note>

## How Puzzles Work

<Steps>
  <Step title="Initial Position">
    Each puzzle starts with an opponent's move already played. You need to find the best continuation.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Find the Best Move">
    Calculate the best move or sequence. You may need to find multiple moves in a row.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Opponent Responds">
    The puzzle automatically plays the opponent's best defensive moves.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Complete the Solution">
    Continue until you've completed the entire tactical sequence.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Puzzle Features

### Puzzle Rating System

Every puzzle has its own rating calculated using the Glicko-2 system:

- Puzzles start with an estimated rating based on the game they came from
- Rating adjusts based on player success/failure rates
- Your puzzle rating is separate from your game rating
- Solve harder puzzles to increase your puzzle rating faster

### Your Puzzle Rating

You have a personal puzzle rating that:
- Starts around 1500 for new accounts
- Increases when you solve puzzles (especially higher-rated ones)
- Decreases when you fail puzzles
- Helps match you with appropriately difficult puzzles

<Note>
  Puzzle ratings are deliberately separate from playing ratings since tactical ability and overall chess strength are different skills.
</Note>

## Puzzle Themes

Lichess categorizes puzzles into 100+ tactical themes, allowing focused training:

### Tactical Motifs

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Mating Patterns">
    - **Back Rank Mate** - Checkmate on the opponent's back rank
    - **Smothered Mate** - Knight delivers mate to a king blocked by its own pieces
    - **Anastasia's Mate** - Knight and rook coordinate for mate
    - **Arabian Mate** - Knight and rook deliver mate in the corner
    - **Opera Mate** - Rook and bishop deliver mate along diagonal
    - **Mate in 1, 2, 3, 4, 5** - Forced checkmates in N moves
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Tactical Devices">
    - **Fork** - One piece attacks multiple targets
    - **Pin** - Piece cannot move without exposing a more valuable piece
    - **Skewer** - Reverse pin forcing a valuable piece to move
    - **Discovered Attack** - Moving one piece reveals attack from another
    - **Deflection** - Force a piece away from defending something
    - **Attraction** - Force a piece to a bad square
    - **Clearance** - Clear a square or line for another piece
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Advanced Tactics">
    - **Sacrifice** - Give up material for positional/tactical advantage
    - **Zwischenzug (Intermezzo)** - In-between move that changes evaluation
    - **Interference** - Block a line between two opponent pieces
    - **Quiet Move** - Strong move without checks or captures
    - **Defensive Move** - Find the only defense
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Game Phase">
    - **Opening** - Tactics in the opening phase
    - **Middlegame** - Most common puzzle category
    - **Endgame** - Endgame tactics and technique
    - **Rook Endgame, Queen Endgame**, etc. - Specific endgame types
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Material Outcome">
    - **Advantage** - Gain a significant advantage
    - **Crushing** - Achieve an overwhelming position
    - **Equality** - Find the equalizing move
    - **Promotion** - Advance a pawn to promotion
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Special Categories

- **Master** - Puzzles from master-level games (2300+ rating)
- **Master vs Master** - Both players were masters
- **Super GM** - From games between elite grandmasters
- **Long** - Complex puzzles requiring multiple moves
- **Short** - Quick tactical strikes (1-2 moves)
- **One Move** - Single-move puzzles

## Puzzle Modes

### Puzzle Dashboard

Access your personalized puzzle dashboard to:
- View your puzzle rating and performance history
- See your accuracy by theme
- Track puzzles solved per theme
- Identify your tactical strengths and weaknesses
- Choose specific themes for targeted practice

### Puzzle Streak

How many puzzles can you solve in a row?
- Solve consecutive puzzles without mistakes
- Builds from easier to harder puzzles
- Global leaderboard tracks the longest streaks
- Different from rated puzzles - purely for fun and challenge

### Puzzle Storm

Speed-solving mode:
- Solve as many puzzles as possible in 3 minutes
- Start with easy puzzles, difficulty increases
- Score based on number solved
- Leaderboards for daily, weekly, and all-time

### Puzzle Racer

Race against opponents:
- Competitive puzzle solving
- First to complete 20 puzzles wins
- Real-time racing against other players
- Requires both speed and accuracy

## Opening-Based Puzzles

Train tactics in specific openings:

- Filter puzzles by opening family or specific variation
- Examples: Sicilian Defense, Italian Game, Queen's Gambit
- Helps you learn typical tactics in your opening repertoire
- Combines opening knowledge with tactical awareness

<Note>
  Opening-based training is especially useful for understanding typical tactical patterns in positions you regularly reach.
</Note>

## Puzzle Interface

### Controls

- **Move the pieces** - Drag and drop or click-click to submit moves
- **Hint** - Get a subtle hint without seeing the full solution
- **Give Up** - Reveal the solution (counts as a failed attempt)
- **Continue** - After solving, move to the next puzzle
- **Retry** - Attempt the puzzle again from the start

### Feedback

- **Correct move** - Green checkmark, puzzle continues
- **Wrong move** - Red X, can retry or see solution
- **Alternative move** - "Good but not best" for valid alternatives
- **Puzzle complete** - Rating change displayed

### Information Displayed

- Puzzle rating and your rating
- Number of plays this puzzle has received
- Themes associated with the puzzle
- Source game (link to the original game)
- Vote buttons to rate puzzle quality

## Puzzle History

Review your past puzzle attempts:
- Browse all solved and attempted puzzles
- Filter by outcome (solved/failed)
- Re-attempt puzzles you previously failed
- Track your improvement over time
- Export puzzle data for analysis

## Puzzle Quality

### Voting System

Help improve puzzle quality:
- Vote thumbs up for good puzzles
- Vote thumbs down for unclear or incorrect puzzles
- Community voting helps filter high-quality puzzles
- Poorly-rated puzzles appear less frequently

### Puzzle Generation

Puzzles are automatically generated:
1. Server analyzes completed games with Stockfish
2. Identifies positions where one move is significantly better
3. Verifies the solution is forced or clearly best
4. Tags puzzles with appropriate themes
5. Assigns initial rating based on game strength and difficulty

<Note>
  New puzzles are constantly added as games are played and analyzed on Lichess.
</Note>

## Anonymous Puzzle Play

Even without an account:
- Solve unlimited puzzles
- No rating tracking (puzzles are randomly selected)
- Progress not saved between sessions
- Create an account to track improvement
