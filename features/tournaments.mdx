## Overview

Lichess hosts thousands of tournaments daily, from hourly blitz arenas to prestigious titled championships. Tournaments provide competitive chess with rankings, prizes, and the thrill of multi-game competition.

## Tournament Types

### Arena Tournaments

The most popular format on Lichess:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Join Anytime">
    Enter or leave at any point during the tournament
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Continuous Pairing">
    System pairs you with new opponents automatically
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Score Points">
    Win, draw, or lose games to earn arena points
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Rankings Update Live">
    Leaderboard updates in real-time throughout the tournament
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Arena Scoring:**
- Win = 2 points
- Draw = 1 point
- Loss = 0 points
- Win streak bonus: +2 points for double wins, +4 for triple wins, etc.
- **Berserk bonus**: Half your clock at game start for 1 extra point

<Note>
  Arena tournaments allow you to take breaks, leave early, or join late while still competing for ranking position.
</Note>

### Swiss Tournaments

More structured format similar to over-the-board tournaments:

- Fixed number of rounds (typically 5-11)
- All players play the same number of games
- Swiss pairing system matches players with similar scores
- Must be present for all rounds
- 1 point for win, 0.5 for draw, 0 for loss
- Tiebreaks: Buchholz, Sonneborn-Berger

**Best for:**
- Players who want predictable tournament length
- Fair competition with fewer rating mismatches
- More traditional chess tournament experience

<Accordion title="Shield Tournaments">

  - One per variant per month

  - Once per year for major variants
  - Duration: 4-10 hours
  - Prestigious victories

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Marathon Tournaments">
  Endurance events:
  - Last 24 hours
  - Test stamina and consistency
  - Experimental marathons also exist (4 hours)

## Time Controls

Tournaments span all speed categories:

### Speed Categories

- **UltraBullet**: 15+0 (15 seconds total)
- **HyperBullet**: 30+0
- **Bullet**: 1+0, 2+1 (1-2 minutes)
- **SuperBlitz**: 3+0, 3+1
- **Blitz**: 3+2, 5+0, 5+3 (3-8 minutes total)
- **Rapid**: 10+0, 10+5, 15+10 (8-25 minutes)
- **Classical**: 30+0, 30+20 (25+ minutes)

<Note>
  Tournament time controls are carefully chosen based on the tournament duration and frequency to ensure fair competition.
</Note>

## Creating Custom Tournaments

### As a Regular User

Create tournaments for teams, friends, or clubs:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Tournament Page">
    Click "Tournament" → "Create a new tournament"
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Configure Settings">
    - Tournament name
    - Time control and increment
    - Duration (20-720 minutes)
    - Start time (or immediate)
    - Rated or casual
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Set Conditions (Optional)">
    - Rating range requirements
    - Minimum rated games played
    - Team membership requirement
    - Password protection
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Advanced Options">
    - Enable/disable berserk
    - Enable/disable chat
    - Add description
    - Set position for thematic tournament
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Tournament Conditions

Control who can join:

- **Minimum/Maximum Rating**: Restrict rating range
- **Minimum Rated Games**: Require experience (prevents new accounts)
- **Team Battle**: Restrict to team members
- **Account Age**: Require accounts older than X days
- **Title Requirement**: Only titled players
- **Password**: Private tournaments with entry code

<Note>
  Conditions help ensure fair competition and prevent rating manipulation from new or suspicious accounts.
</Note>

## Team Battles

Special tournament format for team competition:

- Multiple teams compete simultaneously
- Players earn points for their team
- Team leaderboard shows team rankings
- Individual leaderboard shows best players
- Often sponsored with prizes
- Great for team spirit and club competitions

**How it works:**
1. Must be member of a participating team to join
2. Play normal arena tournament games
3. Your points contribute to your team's score
4. Winner determined by team total points

### Berserk Mode

Risk-reward mechanic available in most Arena tournaments:

- Click the Berserk button before making your first move
- Your clock is cut in half
- Win = +3 points instead of +2 (1 bonus point)
- Lose = still 0 points
- Cannot berserk on first game

**Strategy:**
- Berserk when you have a significant advantage
- Avoid berserk in equal positions
- Good players can gain significant point bonuses

<Note>
  Some tournaments disable berserk (shows as "no berserk" in tournament info).
</Note>

### Streak Scoring

Win consecutive games for bonus points:

- 2 wins in a row: +2 bonus (4 points for 2nd win)
- 3 wins in a row: +4 additional bonus (6 points for 3rd win)
- 4+ wins: continues scaling
- Streak breaks on loss or draw

### Entry Requirements

- Rating requirements (if any)
- Minimum games played requirement
- Time control (can you commit?)

## Fair Play in Tournaments

Lichess enforces strict fair play:

- **Engine detection**: Statistical analysis identifies cheating
- **Multi-accounting**: Duplicate accounts banned
- **Rating manipulation**: Suspicious patterns investigated
- **Entry restrictions**: New accounts restricted from some events

Violators are removed and banned from future tournaments.
